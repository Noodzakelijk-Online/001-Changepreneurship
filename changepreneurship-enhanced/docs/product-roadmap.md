# Changepreneurship Product Roadmap

## Positioning Shift

Changepreneurship should move from being perceived as an AI-powered assessment tool to being understood as a decision system for founders.

The product should help founders answer five high-value questions:

1. Which idea should I pursue?
2. Why is this idea stronger or weaker than my alternatives?
3. What is the biggest risk in my current direction?
4. What should I do next, in practical terms, this week?
5. How do I know whether I am improving over time?

That shift matters because users do not pay for assessment alone. They pay for better decisions, lower risk, clearer execution, stronger conviction, and artifacts they can use with cofounders, mentors, or investors.

## Strategic Goal

Build Changepreneurship into the operating system for early-stage founder decision-making.

The end-state product should combine:

- Founder profiling
- Idea evaluation
- Venture-fit scoring
- Risk mapping
- AI-guided execution planning
- Progress tracking over time
- Collaboration and shareable outputs

## Core Product Thesis

The strongest moat is not UI and not generic AI output. The moat is the combination of:

- Structured founder data across multiple dimensions
- Proprietary founder-to-venture-fit logic
- Benchmarking against similar users and trajectories
- Explainable scoring, not black-box scoring
- Continuous reassessment and progress deltas over time
- Workflow outputs that move users from insight to execution

## Product Principles

Every major feature should satisfy at least one of these principles:

1. Reduce founder uncertainty.
2. Improve decision quality.
3. Turn insight into action.
4. Make progress measurable.
5. Produce outputs that can be shared or reused.
6. Increase retention through longitudinal value.

## Product Pillars

### 1. Decision Support

The platform should help users compare paths, not just reflect on one path.

Key capabilities:

- Compare multiple startup ideas side by side
- Show founder-market fit and venture fit by idea
- Highlight strongest and weakest dimensions per idea
- Recommend the best next bet, with reasoning

### 2. Guided Execution

Users should leave each phase with a clear action plan, not only a score or narrative summary.

Key capabilities:

- Weekly action plans
- Validation roadmaps
- Templates and execution packs
- Success criteria for each suggested action

### 3. Explainable Intelligence

AI output must feel reasoned, not decorative.

Key capabilities:

- Evidence-based scoring
- Score drivers and detractors
- Risk explanations
- AI copilot support inside each phase

### 4. Longitudinal Growth

The product should become more valuable over time.

Key capabilities:

- Reassessment after 30 days
- Trend tracking
- Progress deltas
- Benchmarking over time

### 5. Collaboration and Utility

The output should be useful beyond the product itself.

Key capabilities:

- Shareable reports
- PDF exports
- Investor brief
- Cofounder alignment report
- Advisor summary
- Team collaboration

## Recommended Feature Roadmap

## Phase 1: From MVP to Decision Product

### Goal

Move from static assessment and AI insights into actionable founder decision support.

### Key outcomes

- Users can understand which idea is strongest
- Users can see why a score is high or low
- Users receive a concrete next-step plan
- Product value becomes clearer and easier to explain

### Features

#### 1. Idea Comparison Workspace

Allow users to compare two to five startup ideas side by side.

Core experience:

- Create and name multiple ideas
- Run idea-specific evaluation flows
- Compare ideas across shared scoring dimensions
- Show a recommendation: best long-term fit, best short-term validation candidate, best monetization candidate, lowest-risk candidate

Why it matters:

- Founders rarely struggle with having no idea
- They struggle with choosing among competing ideas
- This makes the product more decision-oriented and more valuable than a typical assessment tool

#### 2. Evidence-Based Scoring Layer

Introduce a transparent scoring model with clear dimensions.

Suggested dimensions:

- Founder-Idea Fit
- Founder-Market Fit
- Market Clarity
- Customer Pain Strength
- Differentiation Strength
- Monetization Readiness
- Execution Readiness
- Overall Risk

For each score, display:

- Score value
- Short explanation
- What increased the score
- What decreased the score
- What the user can do to improve it

Why it matters:

- Converts AI output into something users trust
- Makes the product feel systematic and premium
- Creates a reusable framework for benchmarking and advisor workflows

#### 3. Dynamic Action Engine

Generate a practical action plan based on the user’s weakest dimensions.

Examples:

- Weak market clarity -> do 10 customer interviews
- Weak monetization readiness -> define pricing hypotheses and run price-sensitivity validation
- Weak differentiation -> map competitors and rewrite positioning
- Weak execution readiness -> define 30-day execution plan and first milestone

Each action should include:

- Why this action matters
- What to do
- Deliverable expected
- Success criteria
- Estimated time

Why it matters:

- Translates insight into behavior
- Increases repeat usage
- Gives the product a coach-like value layer

#### 4. Templates and Execution Packs

For each recommended action, provide structured templates.

Examples:

- Customer interview script
- ICP worksheet
- Problem statement template
- Value proposition worksheet
- Pricing hypothesis worksheet
- Competitor analysis template
- Validation sprint template
- Go-to-market checklist

Why it matters:

- Saves users time
- Makes outputs usable immediately
- Adds tangible value beyond analysis

## Phase 2: Build a True Moat

### Goal

Differentiate the product through proprietary matching logic and longitudinal intelligence.

### Key outcomes

- Product becomes more personalized and harder to replicate
- Users see how they compare to relevant founder cohorts
- Product gains retention loops

### Features

#### 5. Founder Archetype + Venture Fit Matrix

Extend the founder archetype into a venture-fit engine.

The system should answer:

- What types of businesses fit this founder best?
- What business models are a bad fit?
- What kind of cofounder or operator would complement them?
- What blind spots are most likely to hurt them?

Possible venture-fit outputs:

- Best suited for B2B services
- Strong potential for niche SaaS
- Weak fit for heavily regulated ventures
- Weak fit for capital-intensive consumer hardware
- Needs strong operational cofounder

Why it matters:

- Gives the product a strong identity
- Moves beyond generic personality typing
- Feels uniquely relevant to early-stage founders

#### 6. Benchmarking Layer

Benchmark the user against relevant cohorts.

Examples:

- Compared to founders with similar archetypes
- Compared to users building in similar market categories
- Compared to founders at the same stage of venture maturity

Display:

- Above average / below average by dimension
- Top strengths vs cohort
- Biggest gap vs cohort
- Benchmark movement over time

Why it matters:

- Makes scores more meaningful
- Creates social and comparative context
- Increases perceived sophistication and credibility

#### 7. Decision Support Over Time

Introduce reassessment and trend tracking.

Core features:

- 30-day reassessment prompt
- Progress delta dashboard
- Dimension-by-dimension movement
- “What improved” and “what stalled” summary
- Updated action plan after each reassessment

Why it matters:

- Builds retention
- Makes the product feel like an operating system, not a one-time assessment
- Encourages founders to use the system throughout early execution

## Phase 3: Outputs People Can Use Outside the Product

### Goal

Turn internal insight into external utility.

### Key outcomes

- Users can take outputs into meetings and real workflows
- Product becomes easier to monetize
- Value becomes more concrete and business-facing

### Features

#### 8. Shareable Outputs

Create export-quality outputs for different use cases.

Recommended artifacts:

- Founder report PDF
- Venture viability brief
- Cofounder alignment report
- Advisor summary
- Investor-ready one-page memo
- 30-day validation roadmap export

Why it matters:

- Users pay more readily for outputs they can use immediately
- Raises product professionalism
- Supports B2B, coaching, and advisory use cases

#### 9. Business Brief Builder

Generate a concise strategic brief from completed assessments.

Sections:

- Founder profile
- Opportunity statement
- Target customer
- Core problem
- Proposed solution
- Value proposition
- Monetization model
- Key risks
- 30-day validation plan

Why it matters:

- Bridges the gap from thinking to communicating
- Helps users discuss the idea with others
- Improves usefulness for mentors and investors

## Phase 4: Workflow Product, Not Just Solo Tool

### Goal

Expand from solo founder introspection into team and advisor workflows.

### Key outcomes

- Higher willingness to pay
- Better retention
- Broader use cases

### Features

#### 10. Collaboration Mode

Enable multi-user workflows.

Roles:

- Founder
- Cofounder
- Advisor
- Mentor

Capabilities:

- Shared reports
- Comments on recommendations
- Compare cofounder profiles
- Detect alignment gaps
- Assign actions to collaborators

Why it matters:

- Converts the product from a single-user tool into a team workflow
- Increases stickiness
- Opens higher-value plans and B2B partnerships

#### 11. Cofounder Alignment Module

Let two founders compare answers and operating preferences.

Show:

- Shared values
- Strategic disagreements
- Risk tolerance mismatch
- Execution style mismatch
- Role fit recommendation

Why it matters:

- Very high perceived value
- Highly shareable
- Difficult to replace with generic AI tools

## Cross-Cutting Feature: AI Copilot Inside Each Phase

This should not be treated as a separate final-page feature. It should be integrated throughout the experience.

Use cases:

- Help me answer this better
- Show me an example of a strong answer
- Rewrite my ICP more clearly
- Challenge weak assumptions in my response
- Suggest better positioning language
- Point out what I have not thought through yet

Why it matters:

- Increases completion rate
- Improves input quality
- Makes final outputs stronger
- Gives the product daily-use feel instead of static-report feel

## Recommended Business Logic Enhancements

## 1. Scoring should be explainable

Every score should have:

- Weight
- Driver inputs
- Confidence level
- Improvement actions

This avoids the product feeling arbitrary.

## 2. Recommendations should be conditional

Different founder profiles should receive meaningfully different next steps.

Bad example:

- Every user gets the same generic “do customer interviews” advice

Good example:

- Visionary founder with weak execution -> focus on operational planning
- Analytical founder with weak market empathy -> focus on interviews and observation
- Strong founder fit but weak monetization -> prioritize pricing and willingness-to-pay validation

## 3. The product should maintain memory over time

The system should know:

- What the user said before
- What actions were recommended before
- What changed since last reassessment
- Which risks remain unresolved

That memory is a major part of the product’s long-term value.

## 4. Outputs should map to user jobs-to-be-done

Different users need different outcomes:

- Solo founder -> “What should I do next?”
- Exploring founder -> “Which idea should I pursue?”
- Cofounders -> “Are we aligned?”
- Advisor -> “Where is this founder stuck?”
- Investor-facing founder -> “Can I summarize this venture clearly?”

1. Idea Comparison Workspace
2. Dynamic Action Engine with templates
3. Founder Archetype + Venture Fit Matrix

That combination creates the clearest product differentiation and moves the platform furthest away from generic AI business tooling.

## Final Recommendation

Do not frame the next stage of Changepreneurship as “more features after the MVP.”

Frame it as the transition into a founder intelligence platform:

- It helps founders choose better opportunities.
- It explains why one direction is stronger than another.
- It identifies the highest-risk assumption.
- It converts insight into action.
- It measures whether the founder is actually improving over time.

That is where the real value, defensibility, and monetization power come from.
